A Legacy Brewed in Ethiopia

Tomoca Coffee has been the heartbeat of Addis Ababa's coffee culture since 1953. Born from a passion for the perfect cup, we source only the finest Ethiopian Arabica beans — hand-picked from the highlands of Yirgacheffe, Sidama, and Harrar — then roasted in-house with decades of tradition. Every sip is Ethiopia in a cup.

Our History

Tomoca was founded in 1953 by an Italian entrepreneur who fell in love with Ethiopia's extraordinary coffee. Nestled on Wavel Street in the heart of Addis Ababa, it became the city's first modern espresso bar — a cultural landmark where diplomats, artists, and everyday Ethiopians shared the same bench and the same cup.

Over seven decades, Tomoca has remained family-run, roasting beans the same way it always has — in-house, in small batches, with the same vintage Italian drum roaster that has been turning since the day it opened.

Today, Tomoca is not just a coffee shop. It is a piece of Ethiopian heritage — one of the country's oldest continuously operating businesses and a symbol of the rich coffee culture that Ethiopia gifted to the world.
